Kananga

Kananga is the capital city of the Kasai-Central province in the Democratic Republic of the Congo. It is located in the south-central country, along the Lulua River. Kananga is a major administration, economic, and cultural center in the region, with a population of over one million people. The city was orginally known as Luluabourg during the colonial period under Belgian rule, but it was named Kananga after the country gained independent in 1960. The city is notable for its strategic location, serving as a hug for transportation and trade within the DRC.
